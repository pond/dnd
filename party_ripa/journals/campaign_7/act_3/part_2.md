# RIPA: Act 3 Part 2
## Session 87 (2026-04-07)
### Campaign Day 37 / Act 3 Day 1, The Outlands, At Our Keep

Urth has picked up Kas's sword. The group has ended up in a rather odd conversation with it. He makes another attempt to get information... WIS 16+2+Guidance 2+Aura 5 save made! We have **five pieces** of information now. It's a jarring encounter this time and Urth drops the sword in shock. Given that reaction, we decide that 5 pieces of information is more than enough!

* The sword has been there for most of the history of Vecna and Kas
* The sword fears what Vecna has decided to do and decided a long time ago that Vecna had to die - just too dangerous; worked over a century to slowly turn Kas against Vecna
* In that process, the sword realised that Kas was hard to create - because Kas is a splintered soul; the sword need to "corrupt" a creature from good to evil to gain great power, but the good was gone from Kas; only the evil remained; the good part was separated
* It believes that the other version of Kas is weak and unimportant - it believes the same is true for Vecna too...
* ...but while that good part is weak, it's **the key to his immortality** - it suspects that Vecna's good part of his soul was put into a phylactory, pushed somewhere that cannot be found and now Vecna has "escaped the bonds of destiny" (claims the sword) and is beyond the gods of fate (apparently some primordial gods of all reality?)

Ah, so that good part of Kas is *not* so important!

* Penny thinks the sword should give us more information without coercion if it's that important! CHA save - 29, OK; but the sword argues that *it* must be the one to kill Vecna. We could just get information then leave without it. Then it goes on a bit of a rant about how it was wronged and needs revenge... It's actually fairly persuasive!

* Thorin asks why it was helping release the Wolfspider; CHA Save - 17, OK; Miska has enough power to storm Vecna's stronghold - it wields chaos as a weapon! It commands an entire legion of demons. Now that the locks between planes have been broken, the demon legions are not restricted in where they can travel. They can travel anywhere. Kas *could've* led Miska's army to the gates of Vecna's stronghold.

A kinda intense discussion about the sword and more information ensues. Urth figures he's the one with the best chance of resisting it; and none of us can think of a better way. "Show me where we have to go" he insists.

This time it's a CHA save, with Luck, aura and another Guidance - d20 -> 1,10 -> but it still works out as 31, which is very high! The sword tries full-on to just take over him this time. But he resists! His personality and ego pushes back.

* A husk of a ruined city floating in the air
* A ball of light being encased within complex metal rune-covered arms of an armillary sphere that rotate around the light, but on a grand scale, there are *armies* on this structure
* The energy from this ball almost feels like a small sun
* It contains pure chaos, but the potential of all creation; Urth thinks some primordial, very powerful being has been trapped inside and is creating new realities - which Vecna is harnessing
* Closer now... A thin sliver of a reality, almost imperceptible, a demi-plane hidden and tucked away, within this mess of possible realities; and in it, a tiny piece of an old memory
* Vecna is hidden in this old memory, within a demi-plane that hides it

With that Urth drops the sword. He relays the information; Urth HIS 26; hmmm, landmarks in those broken floating parts of city - a donut - it looks like Sigil. Ah! **So that bright light is the thing that's in the middle now, with Sigil floating around it!** Vecna is right there, right now.

Penny is looking at Urth in surprise and simply says, "I'm glad I was wrong" - he notices that her hand was on the sunsword, pulled slightly out of its sheath, glowing brightly.

We agree, finally, that we *must* get hold of Alustriel. We will try and keep within Thorin's staff shielding (which is why she won't have been able to find us!) to avoid scrying, but **we will go to Trade Gate** and get in contact.

Urth wonders about getting hold of Flurth again... But we realise that the Rod can cast Simulacrum and do the same job. And it's a few seconds, rather than 12 hours. Well - *yes* then!

Time to walk.



### Trade Gate

It's *very* busy. We hear *haggling* - that's unusual for Trade Gate! - people near the Sky Port bidding for seats. People are trying to run. We head to our favourite magic shop. There's a sign:

"Due to circumstances, we are no longer buying magic items for cash" - in smaller lettering, "Exchanges are still welcome".

Inside, there are people at the main desk trying to argue that *gems* aren't cash and they should be able to "sell" items for gems. The keep arguing they want to sell at low prices but the shop isn't buying!

Thorin goes to try and find someone to help us *buy* stuff, with the others in tow. Penny meanwhile sticks around - if people want to sell things cheap for cash, maybe *she* is in the market! The person looks like some kind of knight, holding a longsword shimmering with magic that almost seems to have a kind of shield floating around it.

* Thorin is in search of a barrier tattoo.
* Francesc is in search of a bow that might have an ability to do something *other than* piercing damage, since creatures seem so often to be resistant!
* Urth is interested in something that might help restore spell slots, or store spells, but in the end decides he isn't interested in the items on offer.

Francesc eventually finds a remarkable bow. She's seen one before, but back then the price tag was off-putting. Give our current situation - almost unthinkably - the 40,000gp cost isn't so bad! Thorin comes up with the idea of trading his Dragon Scalemail, though; that leaves only a 10,000gp balance. He then chances tactic on his item search; what about a Tome of Understanding, which he might be able to read quickly if he found people to cast time dilation spells? That's 50,000gp. Fine! And with that, Francesc decides to do the same - another 50,000.

On the way out Urth notices something interesting - a harp that almost sings on its own, and sings to him; it lets him cast, once each day, a bevy of spells. Tempting; needs attunement though, and *250,000* is a lot to bear. The shop says that they'll take opals - OK! Done!

Penny is tempted by that sword/shield. It's a good one. But in the end, the recent experience defeating Kas, and the knowledge that Vecna himself is a lich, makes her current sword too good to pass up. She hopes the "knight" sells it to someone in need since it's a remarkable weapon that could do much good; but she is not the right person to buy it.

We move on. We go to a more seedy part of town, heading for a tattooist that can do the barrier tattoo for Thorin. We find the shop and it's run by a Cambian! She's tattooing a goblin with a full back tattoo.

* A full-body tattoo, blood and fire and anger all twisted together - the Blood Fury tattoo - is certainly eye-catching!
* There are a few damage-absorbing tattoos, but they each respond to only one specific kind of damage.
* Or the one he had researched - patterns that look like dragon scales - chest and back, or both arms - the very rare barrier tattoo, increasing AC to 18 and stays on his skin in all forms.
* There's another defensive one - it has lots of abjuration patterns, "The lifewell tattoo" - resistance to necrotic and, if reaching zero hit points, you bounce up to 1 (once per day).
* He sees Ghost Step. 3 charges, recharging daily. A charge makes him incorporeal until the end of his next turn; he has resistance to lots of damage in that time and can move through creatures and solid objects.

All require attunement. Given his high-level wildshape AC, he decides to go for Lifewell, then buy some armour to help in wildshape. Meanwhile the three of us find someone to do Sending for Alustriel; **"We have the thing we were looking for - and much more. Must meet urgently."**

(Thorin nips back to the magic shop and gets some Dwarven half-plate - 65,000gp)

**"Where have you been! We have to discuss so many things. How do I find you?!"**

We have to send another - we say **"Trade Gate"** - and give the name of a pub we've seen in our wandering - "Star Crossed Lovers"; we head there directly. Penny has brought her Steed back, but parks the unicorn outside! The pub is close to the port and very busy, with some kind of *auction* going on for seats on outbound ships.

Francesc and Urth (PER 19) immediately spot Alustriel - she's already there. We get to her, but we need something a bit more private. Urth has the idea of Flurth making a Hut, which coupled with Thorin's staff, is pretty good protection. Alustriel goes first:

* They think they know what Vecna's plan is
* They have the skeleton of a roadmap (!) to defeating him but they're missing something really important (they lost the rod)

We reveal the rod! Alustriel says, "We can neutralise his reality!" - and then she talks about how they've discovered that the Moirai on the plane of Theros were tricked into revealing a secret that let him split his soul ("yes we know!") - the *rod is the key to reaching the memory*. They're trying to get an army together. They want to attack him on three fronts:

* Unravel the lies he has used to cage the core tenet of our "current" reality - **the Lady of Pain** - he has managed to somehow logically embroil her in a maze. She could be one our strongest allies.
* A main assault front, with allies from all planes and times, even as far as the Far Realm, for a frontal assault on the fortress/sanctuary, the device around the Nexus of Creation in the centre of Sigil.
* While that happens, people must infiltrate and go into Sigil, uncovering and unwrapping the layers of creative reality. Peel back the onion layers - find the memory, the happy memory - where he hid the "good" version of himself.

If we intend to keep the rod, then Alustriel *strongly* recommends we join that third thing! As it happens - we were all already thinking that. Unanimous - **we keep the rod and we venture into Sigil**. Sigil still counts as another plane, we're told; Alustriel *does have* a tuning fork for that plane, so she can lend it to us and we can use Planeshift.

Alustriel has a Sending Stone. She patiently explains the use (TL;DR, it's a once-a-day Sending spell). She asks if there's anything else we need; we consider matters of perception and stealth - with that she gives Penny some Boots of Elvenkind.

"I know we haven't always seen eye to eye... But... It doesn't bother me. But I hope you have good, strong reasons to do what you're doing. And I mean really good, really strong reasons. You're going to face the hardest challenges you've faced. You'll question your existence and beliefs, possibly even your understanding of reality itself for a variety of reasons. We haven't been able to tell everyone everything... I regret that... But I don't regret the decisions that I made to get us going. If you *do* harbour resentment against me, we can set it aside until this is done. But then we can have it out, if you wish."

Thorin asks, "Are there any more secrets?"

"We haven't been keeping secrets; but we've been keeping each group siloed. For what it's worth, you have challenged my perception and beliefs - you've done and achieved more than I ever thought was possible when I first met you. And what's more you did most of it without help! So I think you are more than capable of doing anything you set your minds to. Just remember not to lose yourself. That is his tool; that is his greatest weapon. He can *change reality*. So have a very strong grasp on what is real."

Flurth says, "I have a *very* strong grasp on reality."

Penny nods; "I know all about the unicorn goddess, so I do too!"

Alustriel says, "I will see you on the fields of victory!" and leaves.

We return to the keep.



### The Outlands Keep

The window is being repaired! People are worried about us. We wonder who might go with us. One obvious choice would be Mynos. But he's just a child, and it sounds like we're going into *bad* places. Francesc suggests - just asking. And Urth just comes out with it; "Want to go on an adventure, kid?"

Penny ends up it on the line, explaining what we're going to do. But in the end, Urth convinces him!

We think we should get to Sigil but Thorin pushes for a long rest; he burned a lot of magic destroying Kas. There are 6 hours before bed if we do that - Thorin and Francesc are able to get to work on their books!

* Thorin 1 day / 6.5 hours
* Francesc 1 day / 10.5 hours (she started while he was getting his tattoo done)

...and we sleep. Mynos was meditating overnight. Penny spends some time talking to him, filling him in on all the details she can about what we've done and what we think we might face in Sigil.



## Campaign Day 38 / Act 3 Day 2
### Outlands Keep

In the morning, Urth creates a Heroes Feast - we all get 2d10 -> only 2 HP! Still, the other benefits are important. It's cereals with milk and apple slices, banana slices but they're a bit brown; there's some orange juice but it's kinda pulpy and coffee but instant. And it lasts 24h.

Mynos is in desert clothes and is wearing his prayer beads again, and wears wraps around his feet and hands. He casts Aid for 3 people, helping Francesc, Urth and himself gain a further 15 max HP. Urth ritual-casts Telepathic Bond.

We get close, join hands - Mynos will make us invisible when we "land" in Sigil - and Francesc uses Planeshift. Only the rod doesn't do that. Oh. So now we check the Cubic Gate. Yes! That does Sigil. So we use the Gate instead! It does Planeshift; that'll do. We do *not* want a 2-way gate in case of demons on the Sigil-side!



### Remnants of Sigil

Mynos is immediately casting an invisibility effect, via creating a kind of mirage. Group PER, highest is 18. We look around; we're surrounded by - well, a mess. **"It's always ruins!"** opines Thorin - he's not wrong! We're in an alleyway, one side - oh, it's what's left of Alustriel's Sanctum. A tower section is intact but looks very unsteady. The street is therefore familiar, but the area is very strange because of the fractured sections; a street ends in rubble and then just *sky*.

The majority of the buildings around us here are badly damaged. There's a sound of scraping and rustling, like claws being scraped across stone and heavy breathing. It sounds nearby! Urth HIS 31, Penny REL 27; they both knew that demons were likely. Well - probably demons then! **And demons have True Sight**. We need a place to hide. The tower in the sanctum is unsteady, but it's the only cover Penny can see; she points, and we run inside!
