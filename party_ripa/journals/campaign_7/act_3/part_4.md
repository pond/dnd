# RIPA: Act 3 Part 4
## Session 88 (2026-06-19)
### Inside the Nexus Of Creation, Campaign Day 38 / Act 3 Day 2

We have, we think, fallen into the Nexus of Creation and are in one of probably several 'wrapped' realities hiding the lie through which Vecna's good self can be revealed.

It masquerades as an alternative Oerth; a marshy landscape around a destroyed Greyhawke, with Kas's castle, partly in ruins, in the distance. We made our way there, fought our way inside and are now what look like the final stages of combat with a Death Knight that we found in a kind of throne room.

Thorin, still thinking about "find the lie" - no Kas, statues of Vecna, not Kas - calls out, "There's no Kas! He should be on the throne!" - so Thorin runs over to sit in it himself! PER 23. The Death Knight is looking at him, apparently not puzzled, nor disagreeing, but it's driven - driven to defend the room - to kill anything in it. And the Death Knight is really messed up, too; and he hears a sigh, a sort of bored "ugh" *coming from behind a door to his right*. There are the rattling of chains.

He yanks the door open. An almost-square chamber - a cell, basically - in which kneels Kas, arms and legs shackled to a ten-foot long shiny chain made of something clearly more exotic than steel. The chain's attached to an adamantine ball - the sheen of the metal unmistakable, after Penny's armour. Slung on his back is a magnificent-looking sword, a glowing symbol with a hand with eye and crown on top. Vecna's symbol, *plus* a crown. His "sigh" noises seem to be related to remnants of tapestries on the wall, with which he is preoccupied.

Thorin decides to leave the fight to everyone else as this seems more important (and it most likely is) so he goes inside. He asks Kas, essentially, what's going on, but Kas doesn't really respond at first. Seems to have given up all hope. Then he sighs and shrugs. "You make no sense. What's the point of it all?"

The Death Knight looks at Penny and casts Command. Penny WIS save 31 - she resists.

Flurth runs back in again, intending to use Bigby's Hand amongst anything else he has time for. He peers around the corner and tries a crossbow but it's no good; and then a firebolt; no good either. Both just bounce off the Death Knight's armour. Well then - Bigby's Hand it is! 28 to hit, hits - 22 force damage. *Almost* but not quite! The Death Knight is clinging on by a thread. Flurth withdraws.

The Death Knight knows Flurth is just around the corner; and he sees Urth and Penny; he runs towards Urth - **but casts Dispel Magic** in the direction of Flurth! Urth sees it coming and Counterspells, but despite magic resistance, 1 and 3 - fails - Counterspell would work... Celebration... But the Death Knight uses a Legendary Resistance and *forces* the Dispel through. But it'll need a check to defeat the L7 casting of Flurth, leading to a DC17 check - rolls an 8, +5 modifier - **Flurth lives on!**

Penny runs up to flank and attacks, hitting confidently with the Sun Sword; 16 damage is enough and the Death Knight starts to crumble. Arcana checks - Urth and Penny miss, but Flurth notices, that as the armour collapses, there's still a very weak magical field is still holding it together. It's probably a life force of *vengeance*, driven by madness and rage - nothing that can be fixed. *It is only a matter of time before this comes back!*

Penny runs in after Thorin, swearing a bit, wondering where he's gone. She makes it into the doorway. She sees the Kas and sword and exclaims - "but that's bollocks, *we* have the sword!" - Thorin opines that in this reality Vecna has already "won", he's already the king, and that's why Kas has given up. So the two us agree - **the sword is the lie** - we must destroy the sword with the rod. Penny yells back to Francesc - "hurry up, we need the rod!"

Everyone starts heading in, Francesc moving the fastest. As Mynos walks past the remains of the Death Knight and says, "You know that thing's not dead?"

Francesc calls over her shoulder, "Is there a window? Can't you just throw it out of the window?!"

Urth asks Mynos if he knows how long we've got. Mynos opines that the armour is the key; we have at most a few minutes.

There's some debate about what to hit. Kas is kind of blocking his sword; it's up against the wall, on his back. And Penny thinks that this given-up Kas might be the lie, since Kas never did give up. Either could be true... So Francesc just tries whacking Kas with the rod directly! It hits; 9 damage. Tries again - nat 20, a Crit using the Rod of Many Parts as an improvised weapon! - Penny shouts, "Fuck, don't break it!" - but only 7 damage this time.

Kas retaliates as Francesc tries to run. It turns out he has more freedom of movement than she thought! He grabs her and tries to sink in his teeth... 10 piercing, 9 necrotic; HP max goes down by resisted *5* + 9 - 14 loss.

Penny calls Mynos to the doorway - "What's going *on* in here?" "You've engaged in battle with someone else?!" "We're just trying to destroy the lie!" "Maybe the sword?" adds Thorin.

Mynos says - "Kas, can we have your sword?" - and Kas sighs, takes it and casts it on the ground! Then he *picks up* the adamantine ball and walks into an adjacent chamber.

Well maybe next, smash the sword? Thorin asks for the rod and tries to strike the crown on the sword. CON Saves all round!

Thorin ARC 23 realises that time isn't passing *except* for us. This strange stasis effect is all around us, effectively everywhere *else*; eventually time will restart, but we're OK. We're still moving.

To Penny's astonishment, Frances and Thorin talks about starting to read their magical books!

Fate die: D8->4; another roll; D8->2. Six hours pass. As Thorin and Francesc read, Penny - very conscious that *nobody gives a shit that she's down well over 100hp* - self-heals as best she can (14 d10 hit die, gets *almost* healed!)

**Reality starts to warp and twist again...**

Yelling, shouting, a whole lot of noise and movement around us. We're on the edge of some kind of village, town or city square. It looks like some kind of public execution! Four huge gallows.

Penny ADV PER 19. Behind the gallows, a hall or castle with a great balcony overlooking the square. And there, surprisingly, is a gaunt, sallow, undead-looking creature - reminiscent of the Death Knight - but they *also* look like Lord Neverember! Last thing we knew, Neverember was alive and well after Thay almost managed to take the city, but that was many months ago. She's pretty sure this is Neverwinter, so what's going on?

Francesc is wondering about the four gallows. All four are, sadly, occupied - and Nat 20 insight from Francesc - all druids... They look very like the druids we met in the forest, all that time ago. And there are Red Wizards here too, overseeing things and flanking Lord Neverember! Is the lie that Red Wizards *did* take the city? And the various guards and soldiers around the gallows - *all undead*.

And then she realises; Lord Neverember is stilted in his movements. Stiff. Unusual... As if his body has rigour mortis. And on his forehead she can *just* make out a glowing blood tattoo of - a hand with an eye in the middle. And Neverember's eyes are white, clouded over. He wears a crown that looks *really* similar to the one on the false Kas' sword - the hand and eye in his forehead, and a real crown atop.

We discuss what might be right or wrong. It's been a while since we were at Neverwinter, but that tattoo - seems pretty solid - we wonder if that is the lie? "The tattoo on the king?" asks Mynos. Flurth pipes up - "From what I've read, Neverwinter doesn't have a king!" - Urth looks surprised, *"You read?!"*

Ah, so! No king! That would be the lie. We need to hit that crown with the rod.

We have hurried, but hushed discussions about options. Neverember is some 5-10 feet back from the balcony edge; the edge is 50ft away horizontally, but 40ft up. Around 75ft distance.

Urth gets bored and starts to move smooth through the crowd. INS nat 20. Huh. The crowd is a combination of frightened and angered. Hmm. A riot? A *real* riot could cause Neverember to be simply whisked away. An illusion?

He suggests it. We like that - even if the Red Wizards saw through that with e.g. Truesight, they'd be distracted all the same. Thorin can use the rod to cast Arcane Gate, pass it to Francesc and Francesc can try to hit the crown as soon as through.

### LEVEL 18

...and Urth and Penny *also* get up to level 18, somehow! We all come to. Thorin casts Greater Restoration to sort out Francesc's reduced HPs; Penny brings her up to full health with hands-on. And then we take stock of our surroundings - but we're still in some kind of void.

As our eyes adjust, we realise there *are* forms here. Distant specks of silvery-white and vague textures. We're floating, weightless. This is the Astral Sea, maybe?

"Wait, I always wanted to do this!" says Penny, thinking that farting might equate to thrust - so she does. And maybe it'd work, *were one not wearing a suit of armour*.

Thorin PER 19 spots a slight difference in distant texture. Mountains? He remembers that the Astral Sea works by *thinking* about where you want to and so, he thinks that - and off he goes! Mynos isn't sure what to do, shooting off in an askew direction with a yelp - Thorin hastily throws him a rope to get things back under control. Thorin reminds us *how* to move, and tells us where he's going; we follow.

We're all going at different speeds! It takes a while to adjust. It takes about half an hour for us, as a group, to get to what looked like mountains; as we get closer, though, we can make out more of what we were seeing. It's not just mountains - things are moving - and there's some kind of strange nebula, or astral cloud, behind things, making it clearer. And then... Oh! It's not mountains, it's a *creature!* - some kind of huge one-eyed creature, with a female giant/god in its claw. And Flurth knows what it is! (28). "An astral dreadnaught!" he exclaims - it's kilometres long - and it seems to be **eating petrified remains of gods**.

The "mountain ranges" appear to be bodies of petrified dead gods that this thing is eating.

As we get closer, Francesc looks to see if there's anything we might recognise from before - crowns, symbols of Vecna... PER 28... Nothing. But this creature, this Astral Dreadnaught, looks as if it is in pain - poor health - it leaves behind a trail of puss and blood! The blood is not red, though, but a strange kind of glowing flame. Nonetheless it seems driven to eat these gods; some kind of - well, cosmic recycling, of sorts.

Penny wonders about that and starts to move ahead of the group, but out to the side a bit, wondering what that injury looks like. ADV PER 19 - she spots 6 other creatures, that are very odd. Fleshy pyramids! Each seems to be half made up of a strange toothy maw, with one bloodshot eye at the top; they are biting the Dreadnaught. They're big too, but against the Dreadnaught, they seem pretty small. They seem focused on its stomach area. As it moves, she occasionally *almost* spots something... A flash of light or something? As if through the cuts, some light emanates from within? But it is clearly in a lot of pain and there's - is that a *hilt* of some kind of dagger?

Closer still - the blade of that dagger-like object must be some 20-30ft across.

She turns around to get back to the group, lets them know what she's seen and states the opinion that perhaps we should aim for that dagger thing. Thorin wonders if Vecna wanted this creature dead, to stop it recycling - and perhaps bringing forth new? - gods, and tried to kill it. We agree to examine the dagger. If all else fails, we could just try hitting things with the rod to see what happens!

Closer again, there are big gashes. Thorin PER 30 - **this Dreadnaught is dying**. The pyramid things are scavengers, opportunistic, eating this thing because it's dying - they're not killing it. But what *is* killing it is hard to figure out. Can we get inside? Thorin heads for one of the big gash, but realises that where the blade itself goes in, there are openings we could use.

The blade itself - actually perhaps more of a sword in overall dimensions? - is about 15ft long. The pyramid-like creatures seem to have dimensions of about 10ft a side and we notice that because *they* notice *us* and start to approach!

Our thoughts turn to perhaps trying to pull the sword out again - even though often, the advice for wounds "plugged" by a blade would be to leave the blade in place. It doesn't have a crosspiece at the handle - just a blade then a wrapped handle, some 20ft diameter - and it must be very, very heavy! Levitate wouldn't do it; a rope would break... We don't see how we could do it.

Thorin suggests "going in through the mouth" - we fly up and there's the head of this impossibly huge creature, eating the dead god. We're far to small to be even noticed. When open, the mouth is at least 300ft across, moving slowly. Plenty of time to get in, but a lot of things to dodge from the "crumbs" of the food.

Well then. In we go.

Not that far inside is the "event horizon" to whatever the next dimension would be, but this is definitely some kind of opening; **we allow ourselves to pass through** and it's little different from stepping through a door.

Patches of light, patches of dark. The light comes from unnatural-looking tears in the "edges"/walls; we know those are "leaking" the light *out* elsewhere, but those seems bright *in* here. None of us can make much out (PER low rolls, lots of nat 1s!) except for Urth - PER 20. Mounds of - stuff, churning, turning over on top of each other all the time. Not stable ground for sure! At least we still seem to be moving on "astral sea rules", so we "think to move" and don't need things like Fly spells to keep off the churning 'ground' below.

The light is brightest where the blade has jutted in. We head that way.

A strange rift of sorts, smoky, blue and purple - vertical, insofar as we've decided on up-and-down - with indistinct, an impression of figures behind it, appears to run all the way up and down... *Except* where the blade pierces it. That leaves a clear column behind. We can *almost* make out what might be bright glyphs in the smoky area but can't make out what those are. And the blade goes *through* the barrier.

Francesc realises that she could get a better view *along* the blade so flies up and along its length, Thorin close behind. As she does that, though, more of the pyramidal Eyemongers appear, who were below the blade previously and seem to be curious (though nonetheless very dangerous). Francesc PER 25 then sees a *temple*, of all things. It's covered in glyphs and runes that seem to be protecting something inside. And rising out from the depths below, frightening the Eyemongers away - some kind of Cosmic Horror! It's huge, some 20ft wide and maybe 30ft long. The Eyemongers have picked up speed and are clearly just *fleeing*.

Penny and Mynos are a fair distance away.
