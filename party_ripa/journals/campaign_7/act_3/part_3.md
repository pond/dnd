# RIPA: Act 3 Part 3
## Session 88 (2026-04-28)
### Remnants of Sigil, Campaign Day 38 / Act 3 Day 2

We've gone back to the wreckage of Sigil and are currently hiding inside what's left of Alustriel's Sanctum, as something is approaching and we've assumed it's not friendly!

Thorin sends up a Hawk familiar to scout around; it flies through holes in the structure and sees two large demons - very ugly, winged and misshapen (technically: Vrocks) They appear to be fighting over the remains of a corpse, in a manner somewhat reminiscent of hyenas.

The hawk is scared by that so doesn't see too much (Thorin hawk-PER 12), flying away and up to perch on a building. But from *there* it seems more of the area around. It's all a mess, but there are other demons and a couple of hellhounds around - we're sure that's just the tip of the iceberg.

To get to the nexus, we _must_ fly. Mynos can shield us as we fly, but he'll need to know if anyone is paying attention to us. His illusion works on the mind, not sight, so he can obscure us in the minds of observers, *but only if he knows they're watching* and he can't handle too many at once - in his words, "Don't fly us into a crowded room!". After discussing conventional fly methods, we're worried about the distance to the centre and the time it'd take - in the end Thorin opts for his mist/gas travel spell. We know True Sight would still reveal our true nature; but Mynos can still continue his spell while we're in that form. We have many concerns, not least of which the 1 minute coalescence time whenever we decide to land - and communication in that form, too, so we know *where* to land! Fortunately we still have maybe 50-or-so minutes left of Telepathic Bond.

Thorin is impatient to get moving and, fair enough, analysis paralysis; it seems like our best shot so he casts. A minute later, we're drifting towards the bizarre sight of the sun-like Nexus, surrounded by the vast sweeping metal arms of Vecna's "reality engine". As we get closer, we have to keep recalibrating our concept of size - it's really huge! - and we start to sense the raw use of spellfire and creation of *entire new realities*, being spun out to act in part as shields for the engine that's making them.

Group PER, highest is 22; up towards the centre area is a series of huge platforms in bronze, brass or possibly even gold. It's all very Steampunk! Ornate bannisters, trusses and so-on. The platform at the top has some particularly bright magical aurora effects and, Thorin (who got the 22) sees a figure with which we now have some familiarity; **it's Vecna**...

...but then we see, flying like swarms, groups of demons - some 150 to 200 each! That's an _extremely_ crowded room. There are demonic troops arrayed all over the main structure itself, people who look like cultists, and all manner of other species. **There must be thousands of beings here.**

It's a good kilometre away. There's some discussion about landing on a quiet platform away from the hordes, to assess; but we take a quick vote, Flurth breaking a tie, and decide instead to just gun it - straight for the nexus!

Francesc takes point, Penny trying to assist; ADV SUR 25 (15,15+10) - she notices the ebb and flow of the "flocks" of demons and manages to avoid them as they swoop around, as well as dodging one big swooping arm. Passing by one of the separate, other platforms we *do* see beings on there. We also witness spellfire being pulled in huge jars loaded into wheeled carts, pulling in from a portal - **from Krynn**, Urth sees!

We hit some strange layer of magic that has a very odd effect; it must be some kind of magical guard - WIS saves:

* Penny *with* Heroic, only 17
* Urth 13
* Flurth 14
* Francesc 17
* Mynos (fail)
* Thorin 22

Thorin succeeds - but all of the rest of us fail; we *fall out of cloud form instantly*. We are probably 200-250ft away from the edge of whatever last bit of physical construction is surrounding the Nexus. At least initially we're falling relatively slowly! There's panicked mental communication for a bit. In confusion Penny tries her Steed, but she only has the lower level version available and *that* one can't fly! As the conjured horse starts to plummet, she mentally hastily apologises and dispels it... But it doesn't work that way and the horse, although not physically "real", has a long and unpleasant time in the air before it'll reach the ground many miles below! Thorin says he'll start to transform into himself and as soon as he finishes, he'll turn into a Quetz and catch us. We'll have just started to fall fast by then so it's very, very nerve-wracking; but he *does* manage to catch us. Some of us are in beak and claws, though. Francesc is caught by the beak, Penny by the claws while Urth, Flurth and Mynos get caught on the body. Some damage is still taken by the hard landing.

Mynos continues to do his best to shield us from people watching. Despite this, Thorin-Quetz ADV stealth is only 8! A nearby platform sights us and they start yelling. Francesc immediately fires an arrow and turns one arrow that keeps doubling in number over and over, until it's just a cloud of arrows raining down on the platform. 8d8 force damage for any failed save in the area hit - 32. It takes out more than half of them.

Penny 'thinks' to Thorin-Quetz, from the claw: "GO FASTER".

And he does. We puncture through the strange first veil, with no concentration spell to maintain this time; but then reality itself seems to *warp* - and it feels like our very physical *selves* are warped too!

D6 fate die - 2.

Thorin-Quetz DEX save 22; we've all felt gravity change, especially people on the back. It feels like flying straight into the ground! He pulls up just in time, making a heavy but manageable landing. There are muffled noises from Penny, face-down in the mud still in the claw; for some reason it's very muddy here!

All around seems to be boggy land and mud, with toppled trees; deep red sun, that looks like it might be shining through a haze. The air smells of smoke - and burning flesh. There's a loud "SPLAT!" as Thorin-Quetz lets go of Francesc out of his beak.

HIS rolls... Penny 18. Alustriel said we'd encounter alternate realities and she'd already figured this was one of them. The rod can destroy the 'core lie' at the heart of those realities. It's something out of place. But Urth 30, Flurth 34! They've both seen this place in books... In fact have they *been* here recently? This is Oerth - the *ruins* of Greyhawke City.

So *what* could possibly have changed here? How can we find one tiny thing within an entire reality?

Moreover, Flurth spots in the distance - maybe a couple of hours walk away - a castle of sorts, that overlooks the landscape around, in the foothills of distant mountains. It's ruined compared to when he last saw it, but undoubtedly - **Kas's castle**, from when he used to be human. So this is from when he was a warlord and had just met Vecna.

Thorin-Quetz notices that his mental connection to his Familiar (even though it were a gas cloud) is severed. We're likely on a different plane - or at least something that acts like one.

The group decision seems to be to jump on Thorin-Quetz and fly towards the castle, to see what we can see. We fly over a fairly desolate, ruinous and marshy landscape, the strange dull red sun present the whole time. There's an unsettling similarity in some of the destruction to what we likely did on Eberron. There are things down there, crawling around - body *parts* sometimes - undead.

Thorin-Quetz does think that the way the trees fell seems to be *away from the castle*. He picks up speed.

After a good *hour* of flying... We suddenly realise we're not getting any closer to the castle!

We turn around and spend about 10 minutes flying to the city. It gets larger. In fact, we're *over* the city in about 20 minutes. Thorin-Quetz opines that maybe we just search around here to see if there are any clues - but it's a *city* and in ruins, no less. There aren't the same undead around here though. Within the city walls it's just empty and desolate.

Francesc 24. *No* buildings are left. That's very unusual. Everything is destroyed. And by now, it's been about 90 minutes and the 'sun' hasn't moved. Time may be frozen here, except we *have* seen creatures moving. Thorin-Quetz wonders if the buildings have just *aged*, once we start thinking about things in terms of time passing, or not. There's hardly any stone sticking up from the ground - just a few centimetres of any given structure's lowest level. He deduces that something *did* destroy the city, but it was aeons ago - the ruins are very, very heavily weathered.

Francesc thinks of using the rod for Find The Path to point the way to the castle; perhaps there's a not-obvious route, some winding path, that it might reveal. It *should* show "the fastest way" - and it shoots a beam of light directly at the *sunset*.

We try it! We get back on Thorin-Quetz's back and he flies towards it. As he does so, the space to our left - between us and the castle - just seems to "collapse", getting less and less until the ruins are only about 100ft away. The back is intact, the front is crumbled. **We can see two hulking bipedal creatures, covered in spikes and smeared in blood, walking around in the crumbled front area!**

Thorin turns towards the creatures and Penny fires an energy bolt, just as Penny is going - "oh no, no no no, not that way!" - it seems to hang in the air, about 60ft, in that direction! Penny says, "Fire *that* way - into the sun!" - so she tries it; the bolt goes *sideways* and smashes into a wall. So Thorin-Quetz now flies into the sun and within seconds, we're in melee range.

Initiative!

* Flurth 26
* Penny 22
* Francesc 21
* (Mynos)
* Urth 13
* Thorin 9

We land just in front of the large creatures. Flurth summons L5 Bigby's Hand and he uses a Clenched First attack. Crit! 51 force damage. Then he sends Bardic to Francesc. Penny runs up and hits for 29, then 22 damage.

Francesc uses Hunter's Mark, finding that it's resistant psychic, necrotic, poison and all bludgeoning, piercing and slashing from non-magic or non-adamantine sources. It can't be paralysed, poisoned, charmed, petrified or frightened and has magic resistance! She fires and hits; various components add up to 21 damage; fires again; hits; 12 more damage.

Mynos shoots a bolt of psychic energy at one of the creatures - which by now we've realised are some kind of constructs, metal, shades of Eberron again - and it does nothing. "I can't penetrate their minds!" he complains; "I don't think they *have* minds" replies Thorin-Quezt; "Oh, well, that'd explain it!"

The one nearest Penny sprays a paralysing gas out in a huge cone. CON saves:

* Penny 22 (saves)
* Urth 27
* Thorin 35
* Francesc 19 with luck point
* Flurth and Mynos are safe due to some nearby debris blow

...we all save. No paralysis! Then it attacks Penny but misses, thanks to disadvantage from Sap. But then part of its body separates and appears as a spectre! But now the *other* one moves and uses its spray. CON saves for everyone except Penny:

* Urth 14 (fails)
* Flurth 25
* Monos (fails)
* Thorin 32
* Francesc with luck point, another 19!

Mynos and Urth become paralysed and on his turn, he is unable to break that. Now the detached spectre attacks Penny; it can't get through the armour. And then Thorin-Quetz attacks in turn - he flies around to flank the creature/construct that attacked Penny, but can't get in a hit on the first attempt. Then he turns into... A giant crocodile!

Flurth throws a Firebolt at the one he hit with Bigby's Hand before, but sadly the bolt misses. As a bonus action, he tries another Clenched Fist attack and *this* hits - 21 damage.

Penny attacks with flanking advantage, hitting once for 39 damage with Blinding Smite and it misses its ADV CON save - it's blind! She attacks again, hitting for 13 damage; runs around to use Lay On Hands to free Urth from his paralysis (the Spectre takes an opp attack but misses); then starts to run back for more flanking advantages.

Francesc fires at the construct near the Bigby's Hnad, hits, 25 damage; 6 transferred to the other one; another hit; 21 more damage.

The blinded constructor (these are Cadaver Collectors) swings around randomly and ends up by chance attacking Penny. Matches AC and hits, dealing 24 bludgeoning and 18 necrotic. It continues flailing around, and its second attempt ends up hitting its own spectre and taking it down!

The other Collector moves to attack Francesc. Despite using Bardic to lift her AC, it's unlucky; a d12->7 roll means it *matches* her AC and still hits. 17 necrotic, 17 bludgeoning. Another hit - 17/17 once again. She has some resistances but even so, 51 taken; Penny reacts to use Soothe Pain, reducing that by 11. Now this one spawns spectres - but *three* this time!

Urth attacks the nearest Collector with a Firebolt hitting for 22 fire damage. Then he jumps down off Thorin-now-Croc and moves forward a bit to attack with his Lute of Thunderous Thumping. It hits; 8 + 14 -> 22 damage.

Thorin-Croc repositions himself to once again be flanking with Penny and tries a bite; hits for 30 damage; then gets in another attack for 20 damage - and that does it; the construct falls apart, scattering into various bits of metal.

Spectre A attacks Francesc, with flanking advantage. It hits, doing 10 necrotic; HP max reduces by 10 too! Fortunately her max HP is quite heavily bumped from Aid and Heroes Feast. Another attacks and hits, 6 more lost. The third attacks and hits for 12 as well! But her Magic Absorption feat on each of those turns means there is reduction - 2+3+3 reduction on that (later note: Accidental misuse of that feat, realised much later, but we just run with it)

Flurth moves Bigby's Hand up to the remaining construct, then tries first Firebolt; misses; but does then hit with Clenched Fist for 19 force damage. It's *almost* down. Penny runs up to flank; she attacks and hits, 27 damage; it's down. She's able to reach one of the spectres and take it down with 19 damage, but there are two left.

Francesc transfers Hunter's Mark and point-blank shoes at a spectre. 13 damage. Another hit, 24 damage; it's gone. Only one left.

Mynos has finally shaken off his paralysis; he blasts the remaining spectre with a psychic attack and takes it down.

The others start investigating the courtyard, but Penny takes Mynos aside and checks in. "I'm sorry I couldn't help you sooner. But you seemed safe enough on Thorin's back, and I figured Urth would be more keen to get into the fight. If ever I fear you're in true danger I *will* always do anything I can to help."

The response is a little blunt; "I know; we have done this before! I do not fear."

Francesc self-heals a little. Penny spots some double doors at the far end of the mostly-collapsed room and pushes through; there's a corridor with a series of statues, gold-plated, all of the same human man holding a book and orb. The orb in each case is actually a glass orb. They're clothed (in the carving) and wear a circlet.

Urth/Flurth HIS 34 - This is all Vecna. It's an ode to "good Vecna", we guess. There are inscriptions in an ancient form of Common. Great deeds that Vecna has in theory done. It's either very sweet, or it's all rather overdone. Thorin opines that perhaps given this whole reality is suspect, should we check these out - disbelieve them? Francesc tries tapping with the rod. Nothing. She tries attacking; she is - uuh - proficient in the rod... (!) - she hits, doing 5 damage. Penny is about to laugh as it's pretty meagre, but - WIS saves!

* Penny 16
* Urth 10
* Flurth 12
* Francesc 25
* Thorin 21

12 psychic for failures, else 6. We decide upon strategic withdrawal! We walk up to the next double doors. Francesc is a bit more cautious now and sneaks in, stealth 20, and sees nothing less than a Death Knight! It sees her immediately and its eyes glow a bright yellow, sat upon a throne on a plinth.

Francesc puts up Hunter's Mark; she calls a warning; the rest of us rush in. Initiative from before reused. Hunter's Mark indicates: Immune to poison, necrotic, fear, exhaustion, poisoned condition; no resistances. Flurth keeps back, but sends in Bigby's Hand for another Clenched Fist attack *and another nat 20!* - 10d8 -> 42 damage.

In response, the Death Knight looks around in the room - apparently to sense where Francesc is?! - throwing a Hellfire Orb as close to her as he can! A 20ft radius sphere catches everyone except Urth and Flurth. DEX saves all round:

* Penny 18
* Mynos (fail)

* Penny 3+7 -> 10 (sigh) (fail)
* Mynos (fail)

16 necrotic, max HP down 16 too. Penny runs up and attacks - miss, crit! With L4 Blinding Smite, 14d8+10 -> 78 damage. It fails the saving throw, but chooses to succeed. In reaction it lunges at her with its Dread Blade, but it misses.

Francesc runs in further, takes aim and starts firing. Nat 20! 22 damage. Another hit; 11 more; plus Stasis Strike, 6 more (bit it makes its WIS save). She withdraws out of the room!

The Death Knight acts again; it runs forward and lunges at *Mynos*. Penny reacts to attack; misses (and swears under her breath - there's not much she can do to help Mynos now...) - the Death Knight strikes true and deals 26 damage to the child. "The debt of blood will be paid!" it says, which is a confusing remark!

Mynos tries to push the Death Knight back with a psychic attack; it doesn't resist it and does get pushed back. Mynos starts to run out of the room! As a Sentinel reaction, though, Thorin casts a healing spell on Mynos as he's leaving - L4 Cure Wounds for 51 (!). Urth moves in and tries L4 Dissonant Whispers, WIS save, DC 20; it saves, but still gets 25->12 damage. Then he tries Sacred Flame; but it matches the DC and dodges the damage.
